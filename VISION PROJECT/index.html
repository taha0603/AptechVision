Nutrition Information

What are nutrients and why are they important?

Nutrients are organic substances required for regulating body functions. Following a healthy, balanced diet ensures that our bodies get all the necessary nutrients to function properly and reduces the risk of diseases like heart disease, stroke, type 2 diabetes, and some types of cancer in the long term.

What are starchy foods?

Starchy foods are rich in starch, a type of carbohydrate. This food group includes various grains like rice, bulgur wheat, oats, barley, and rye.

What is fibre?

Dietary fibre is a carbohydrate found naturally in plants that reaches the large intestine intact. Fibre helps maintain a healthy digestive system.

What is protein?

Protein is a macronutrient essential for growth, repair, and maintenance of the body, especially for bones and muscles.

What is sugar?

Sugar is a simple carbohydrate found in different forms. Sugars can be naturally occurring, like those in fruit and milk, or added as ingredients in foods and drinks.

What is fat?

Fats are an important part of a balanced diet. While some fat is necessary for health, excessive fat can be detrimental to overall well-being.

What are vitamins and minerals?

Vitamins and minerals are micronutrients required in small amounts for essential processes in the body. Most of these cannot be produced by the body and must be obtained through the diet.
